  3. Liberica

    4. A rare variety offering a unique, robust profile with smoky, woody, and fruity notes, found primarily in Southeast Asia.

Roast Levels

The degree of roasting significantly impacts flavor, acidity, and body.

  • Light Roast

    • Roasted the shortest amount of time, preserving the bean's original, bright, and fruity or floral flavors with high acidity

  • Medium Roast

    • A balanced option that offers a fuller body and a well-rounded flavor profile with notes of chocolate, caramel, and nuts, and moderate acidity.

  • Dark Roast

    • Roasted until oils surface, resulting in a bold, rich taste with low acidity and smoky or bittersweet notes, and a slightly lower caffeine content per volume

Brewing Methods

Different methods extract flavors in distinct ways

  • Drip/Pour-Over

    • Hot water passes through a filter and grounds by gravity, yielding a clean, light-bodied cup that highlights delicate flavors.

  • French Press

    • Coffee grounds are steeped in hot water and then strained with a metal filter plunger, producing a rich, full-bodied coffee with more oils and sediment.

  • Espresso

    • Hot water is forced through fine grounds under high pressure, resulting in a concentrated shot with a layer of foam (crema).
