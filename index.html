Coffee can be broadly categorized by bean type, roast level, preparation method, and final drink composition, offering a wide array of flavors and experiences.

Coffee Bean Types

The primary coffee species are Arabica and Robusta, with two less common varieties: Liberica and Excelsa.

  1. Arabica

  2. Robusta

    3. A hardier and less expensive bean with higher caffeine content, delivering a strong, bold taste, earthy notes, and a rich crema, often used in espresso blends and instant coffee

  3. Liberica

    4. A rare variety offering a unique, robust profile with smoky, woody, and fruity notes, found primarily in Southeast Asia.

  4. Excelsa

    5. Often classified within the Liberica family, it provides a tart, fruity flavor, adding depth and complexity to blends
